The Rise of Vietnam’s Tech Industry

Vietnam has quickly emerged as a leading tech hub in Southeast Asia, driven by a young, tech-savvy workforce, high smartphone usage, and strong government support. Key initiatives, such as the development of tech hubs and startup incubators, have fostered innovation and attracted foreign tech companies eager to tap into the Southeast Asian market.

IT in Vietnam

The country’s tech startup ecosystem is thriving, with a 38% increase in startups in 2020, alongside a rise in tech unicorns. Vietnam has been recognized as the top startup hub in Southeast Asia, thanks to its growing talent base and venture capital investments, which reached US$741 million in 2020.

Vietnam’s tech sector is a crucial part of its economy, generating US$148 billion in revenue in 2022, with tech exports valued at US$136 billion. The government’s push for digital transformation is also advancing regional cooperation within ASEAN, positioning Vietnam as a digital leader.

Looking forward, Vietnam’s ICT industry is expected to grow by 8% annually from 2022 to 2026, with projections that its digital economy could add US$1 trillion to GDP by 2025. This growth solidifies Vietnam’s status as a key player in the global tech landscape.
